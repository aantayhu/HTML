Security Aspects



However, if the service does finish developing the next few years and is ready for commercial use, what is the most vital concern the public should comprehend before using the service? Amazon Prime Air, like any other digital service, must deal with security concerns, specifically relating to information security and physical safety. Amazon’s approach and designs for the service would not only determine how well these concerns are addressed but also whether customers even consider using the service or not. Since the service is planned to be connected to networks for flight control management and drone-to-drone communication, Amazon Prime Air would be an obvious target for network hackers and drone hijackers (Mac, 2015). Thus, Amazon for the last decade has poured vast amounts of resources into acquiring several network and information security patents to counteract any possible attacks (Michel, 2017). However, this does not guarantee the service’s full security and the company would have to constantly update and develop new defenses against these attacks.

Furthermore, a similar thing can be said for Prime Air’s address of physical security. Patents awarded to Amazon in 2015 explain that the drones would also be imbedded with a series of prime directives and detection devices to avoid colliding with objects, even if the drone loses control or power (Michel, 2017). These patents also present communication systems and distance determining software dedicated to finding alternative paths and landing areas after avoiding those collisions (Michel, 2017). However, just like with information security, Amazon Prime Air cannot fully guarantee zero collisions from occurring. Coding errors along with unique unaccounted circumstances inevitably will emerge. Users of the service would need understand that these problems can only be remedied and never fully solved, that this tradeoff is the price for supposedly more convenience.
