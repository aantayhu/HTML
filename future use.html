Future Use



The future of Amazon’s Drone delivery service is uncertain. Once the trails are completed, the service will have to meet new regulatory standards and restrictions set by the FAA as well as by local and state governments (Amazon, 2020). With gigantic inherent concerns still present and its parent company moving discreetly to compress the project, Amazon Prime Air will likely take more than just a few years to finish developing. Moreover, in its current design, the service will likely only be used in certain circumstances and for certain types of customers. However, due to Amazon’s history of expansion into new markets and applications, the service could, and probably should, also change its approach. Amazon Prime Air could follow the lead of other drone delivery services and concentrate on a more specific market instead of the broader e-commerce world. For example, the service could concentrate on food delivery, or it could even be applied to more lifesaving sectors such as with medical care delivery and search and rescue (Abdelmaboud, 2021).

This evolution into a concentration may ultimately yield better results for Prime Air as one company has proven. In a YouTube video titled “How Rwanda Built A Drone Delivery Service”, the company Zipline is shown to have not only successfully engineered a fleet of plane-like drones that can travel great distances with payloads of over one and a half kilograms but also found new simple methods to vastly decrease loading time, take-off time, and landing time; all of this to instantly deliver vital medical supplies to rural health centers in Rwanda, which currently cannot be viably reached out to by any on-the-ground delivery method (Real Engineering, 2019). Essentially, the service managed to fill a role desperately needed by a certain group of people that only it alone could fill. Although Zipline’s fleet of autonomous drones are designed a little bit differently, service’s innovative methods and market focus were crucial to its success and in part are responsible for the company’s expansion. It is an excellent example of what Amazon Prime Air could turn into and has the best perspective that the service could take for the future. Although the service is far from finished, Amazon Prime Air has changed the discourse of commercial drone delivery technology and has the potential to continue to change it in this decade.
